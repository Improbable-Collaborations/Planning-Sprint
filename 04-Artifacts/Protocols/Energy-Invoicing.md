# Energy Invoicing

*How we track and honour the energy people contribute.*

---

## Status: Draft — informed by Graham's pilot MOU and the Tripod model

---

## Purpose

Create a transparent system for recognising and recording contributions — especially in a volunteer and commons-based environment where traditional invoicing doesn't capture what people actually give. Energy invoicing is not bureaucracy — it's a way to log effort, reflect, and ensure reciprocity.

---

## The Tripod Model

Energy invoicing sits within a broader practice of team health:

| Pillar | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Coherence** | Are the goals clear? Are we aligned on what matters? |
| **Judgment/Autonomy** | Are people empowered to act, seek guidance, and raise flags? |
| **Tending/Review** | Are relationships tended? Is work acknowledged without micromanagement? |

This model is applied at every level — individual, team, and the whole network.

---

## How It Works

### Weekly Energy Reports
- Each Hitchhiker submits a brief weekly voice or written report
- Covers: what was done, energy level, blockers, support needed
- Not a surveillance tool — a reflection and care practice

### Bi-Weekly Check-Ins
- Review of mission progress and wellbeing
- Conducted between Hitchhiker and their mentor/convenor
- Honest dialogue about personal energy, commitments, and health

### Six-Weekly Mission Review
- Formal review at the end of each mission block
- What worked, what didn't, what to carry forward
- Recontracting for the next mission

---

## What Counts as a Contribution?

- Time (hours spent on mission work)
- Expertise (skills applied, knowledge shared)
- Emotional labour (facilitation, conflict resolution, care)
- Resources (tools provided, expenses covered, space offered)
- Outreach (connections made, events organised, recruitment)
