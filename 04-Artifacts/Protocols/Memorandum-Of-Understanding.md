# Memorandum of Understanding — Template

*The framework agreement between a Hitchhiker and Hitchhikers.earth.*

---

## Status: Draft — based on Graham Stewart's pilot MOU (January 2026)

---

## Purpose

This MOU template sets out an evolving framework of mutual commitment between a Hitchhiker and Hitchhikers.earth. It covers onboarding, contribution, rights, IP, funding, and exit. It is a living document, subject to review every 6 weeks.

---

## 1. Parties

- **The Hitchhiker**: Individual contributor and pilot lead for their garden/region
- **Hitchhikers.earth**: The company/project movement, represented by its designated convenors

---

## 2. Duration and Phases

| Phase | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Discovery ("Gift Period") | 6 weeks | Mutual exploration, no deliverables expected |
| Onboarding Mission | 6 weeks | Structured induction, tools, culture, mentoring |
| Active Missions | 6-week blocks (up to 42 weeks/year) | Defined objectives, rest/review between each |

- Annual cycle: ~42 weeks active, ~10 weeks review/integration/rest
- Up to three annual cycles before progression to mentorship
- Renewable/renegotiable every 6 or 42 weeks
- Either party may pause or terminate at end of any 6-week period with 6 weeks notice

---

## 3. Rights on Successful Onboarding (after 12 weeks)

- @hitchhikers.earth email address
- Personal sub-wiki with editing rights
- Use of brand/IP subject to guidelines
- Ability to represent as a Hitchhiker within agreed scope
- Public recognition via onboarding ceremony

---

## 4. Mutual Accountabilities

- Coherence/Trust/Tending tripod practice (see [Energy Invoicing](Energy-Invoicing.md))
- Weekly voice/energy reports
- Bi-weekly well-being and progress check-ins
- Honest dialogue about energy, commitments, health, and resources

---

## 5. Autonomy and Initiative

The Hitchhiker is empowered to:

- Initiate and propose missions, local projects ("gardens"), outreach, and fundraising
- Recruit, onboard, and support other Hitchhikers within their garden
- Seek funding grants and partnerships (major proposals checked with Towel Team)

---

## 6. Intellectual Property and Branding

- Content created by the Hitchhiker is **copyrighted to the creator**
- May be licensed under Creative Commons or similar by mutual agreement
- Hitchhiker branding may only be used for activities within the project
- On departure: right to fork personal materials, but Hitchhikers.earth branding ceases
- Provenance of all work preserved via wiki/journaling — usable in future portfolios

---

## 7. The Public Mask

- Each Hitchhiker develops a public persona ("mask") for stakeholder engagement
- Ceremony including videoed oath, wiki profile, and mission declaration required
- Public representation only after ceremony completion and approval
- Branding may be revoked for severe breach of ethos, after due process

---

## 8. Review and Issue Resolution

### The Tripod Model

| Pillar | Practice |
|--------|----------|
| **Coherence** | Regular clarifying of mission, roles, objectives, priorities |
| **Judgment/Autonomy** | Trusted to act within scope, seek guidance, raise flags |
| **Tending/Review** | 1–6 weekly check-ins on progress, wellbeing, and trust |

### Issue Resolution

- Minor tensions: handled 1:1 (Hitchhiker and mentor/convenor)
- Escalations: Towel Team review panel with clear process
- Good faith and mediation as defaults

---

## 9. Expenses, Funding, and Resources

- Pilot stage: self-financed unless otherwise agreed
- Expenses for tools (Zoom, events) agreed explicitly
- Distributed fundraising with transparent documentation
- Future stipends, salary, or equity arrangements to be agreed in writing
- Risk of unfunded work acknowledged — contributions preserved via wiki/logbook

---

## 10. Structure and Scaling

- Hitchhikers encouraged to form local legal entities ("embassies") as appropriate
- Hitchhikers.earth provides guidance on legal structures and governance models
- Intended model: decentralised ecosystem of gardens/guides/embassies
- Brand standards, mutual aid, and cross-pollination coordinated centrally

---

## 11. Confidentiality and Privacy

- Privacy-respecting methods for check-ins, energy reporting, and sensitive discussions
- Explicit consent required for sharing beyond project team
- Privacy by default and data minimisation

---

## 12. Credit and Departure

- All contributions permanently attributed in the project logbook
- Departing Hitchhikers retain: full credit, ability to carry content forward (with lineage notes), alumni status
- Mutually respectful relationship maintained regardless of outcome

---

## 13. Amendments

- Living document, reviewed every 6 weeks
- Full cycle review after 42 weeks to formalise the next phase
- All amendments signed off by both parties
