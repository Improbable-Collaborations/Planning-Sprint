# Onboarding

*How new Hitchhikers join, orient, and start contributing.*

---

## Status: Draft — drawn from Graham's pilot MOU and onboarding conversations (January 2026)

---

## Purpose

Create a clear, welcoming path for new contributors — modelled on guild traditions, study circles, and the spirit of hitchhiking itself — so that each person finds their place, builds trust, and starts adding value without needing to decode the whole system first.

---

## The Three Phases

### Phase 1: Discovery ("Gift Period") — 6 Weeks

- Understanding the project, its people, and yourself within it
- A mutual tryout: seeing if the chemistry is right
- No explicit deliverables expected — this is a gift of time and attention
- Logged start date, regular check-ins, but low pressure
- Key question: *Is there mutual fit, clear expectations, and aligned intentions?*

### Phase 2: Onboarding Mission — 6 Weeks

- Structured induction: tools, wiki, co-working, culture immersion
- Introduction to the "missions and quests" framework
- Guided mentoring and encounters with other Hitchhikers
- Developing a personal "garden" (area of interest/stewardship)
- Culminates in an onboarding ceremony marking readiness to represent

### Phase 3: Active Missions — 6-Week Blocks (up to 42 weeks/year)

- Work organised in 6-week missions with agreed objectives
- Each mission concludes with a rest/review/recontracting week
- ~42 weeks active per year, ~10 weeks review/integration/rest
- Missions get incrementally more challenging
- After a full cycle (42 weeks): review — continue, pause, change trajectory, or become a mentor
- Maximum of three annual cycles before moving to mentorship roles

---

## What You Receive on Successful Onboarding (after 12 weeks)

- `@hitchhikers.earth` email address
- Personal sub-wiki with editing rights
- Official invitation to use brand/IP (subject to guidelines)
- Ability to represent as a Hitchhiker within agreed scope
- Public recognition within the project (ceremony/ritual)

---

## The Onboarding Ceremony

Completion of onboarding is celebrated by a ritual, including:

- **Videoed oath** acknowledging project values, public/private mask differentiation, and readiness to represent
- **Hitchhiker homepage/profile** completed and approved on the wiki
- **Declaration of initial mission** and visibility rights
- Possible physical tokens (towel, dressing gown)
- Public persona developed in consultation with review panel/Towel Team

---

## The Public Mask

Each Hitchhiker creates a public persona ("mask") for outward engagement:

- Clearly defined scope of representation
- Developed in consultation with Vision Fish/Towel Team
- Submitted for peer review and attestation
- Private face remains guarded — privacy protected through digital tools
- Mask/branding may only be used after ceremony completion

---

## What Missions Can Include

- Building/curating guide content (e.g. Hitchhiker's Guide to Catalonia)
- Event organisation
- Tool building (digital: wiki, vibe coding, apps)
- Fundraising and partnership development
- Outreach and public engagement
- Workshops and training
- Recruitment of new Hitchhikers
- Documentation (journaling, podcasting)

---

## The Garden Model

Each Hitchhiker cultivates their own "garden" — an area of stewardship:

- **Personal gardens**: themes or localities (e.g. gamification, Catalonia, youth)
- **Fundraising**: empowered to seek financing within agreed parameters
- **Events**: running decentralised or personal events
- **Recruitment**: bringing in new people through mentorship and outreach
- Within their garden, Hitchhikers recruit, onboard, and support others

---

## Inspiration: Historical Models

### German Guild System (Gesellenwanderung)
- Tradespeople left home for three years to learn across Europe
- Wore distinct costumes, lived off community goodwill
- Required attestation, logbooks, and rituals
- Year 1: local work/learning → Year 2: Europe-wide → Year 3: global

### Swedish Study Circles
- Collective, peer-driven learning
- Self-organising small groups
- Democratic participation and shared facilitation

---

## Review and Progression

- Formal review at the end of every 6-week mission
- Well-being checks every two weeks
- Weekly voice/energy reports
- After 42 weeks: full cycle review
- Progression path: contributor → mentor → Vision Fish → Towel Team
- Both parties may pause/terminate at end of any 6-week period with 6 weeks notice

---

## Supporting Diversity

- Designed for both extroverts (visible, event-hosting "mobile hitchhikers") and introverts (virtual, home-based "quiet hitchhikers")
- Adaptable tools for check-ins, stress management, and wellbeing
- Intentionally supports neurodiversity
- Multiple valid ways to contribute
