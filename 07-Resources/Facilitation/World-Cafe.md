# World Café

**Juanita Brown & David Isaacs, 1995**

---

Structured conversation in small rotating groups (4–6 people) around café-style tables, with tablecloths participants write and draw on. After several rounds, hosts stay while guests move, cross-pollinating insights. A final harvest brings patterns back to the whole group.

## Strengths

- Extremely accessible and replicable
- Low threat level — the café metaphor is warm and familiar
- Excellent for harvesting distributed knowledge
- Works cross-culturally more easily than most

## Weaknesses

- Can produce a false sense of depth — nice conversations, thin outputs
- Dependent on good questions and skilled hosting
- The "harvest" phase is often under-designed
- At very large scale (500+) it loses intimacy without losing complexity

## Eastern Fit

High. The conversational small-group format resonates across most cultural contexts. Has been widely used in Japan, India, Brazil, and Africa.

## H2GE Relevance

High. The café metaphor maps directly to Restaurant at the End of the Universe events. Rotating conversations with tablecloth documentation is practically a Hitchhiker event already. Strong candidate for the food-centred event format David is developing.
