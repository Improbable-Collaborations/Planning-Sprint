# Dragon Dreaming

**John Croft, influenced by Indigenous Australian traditions**

---

A project design method with four phases — Dreaming, Planning, Doing, Celebrating — arranged in a circle. Strongly influenced by the Gaia Foundation and permaculture thinking. Emphasises that 50% of a project's energy should be in celebration and that projects should serve self, community, and Earth simultaneously.

## Strengths

- Uniquely holistic
- The celebration emphasis corrects a major blind spot in Western facilitation
- Strong ecological and Indigenous grounding

## Weaknesses

- Less tested at large organisational scale
- Can feel idealistic
- Practitioner community is smaller

## Eastern Fit

High conceptual resonance with cyclical, non-linear Indigenous and Vedic frameworks.

## H2GE Relevance

High. The four-phase circle maps to our mission cycles (Dream the mission → Plan → Do → Celebrate/Review). The insistence on celebration aligns with Towel Day, the AGM, and the whole Don't Panic ethos. The triple service (self, community, Earth) mirrors the MOU's garden model.
