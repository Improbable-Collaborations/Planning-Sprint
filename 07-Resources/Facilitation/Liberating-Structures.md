# Liberating Structures

**Henri Lipmanowicz & Keith McCandless, 2010s**

---

A menu of 33 microstructures (1-2-4-All, TRIZ, Troika Consulting, etc.) designed to replace conventional presentation-and-discussion formats. The key insight is that all facilitation involves microstructures — the question is whether they're conventional (default/controlling) or liberating (inclusive/generative). Can be combined in "strings" for complex interventions.

## Strengths

- Immediately practical
- Modular and combinable
- Huge practitioner community
- Free and open-source in spirit
- Excellent for organisations that can't commit to large retreats

## Weaknesses

- Can become a toolbox without a theory of change
- The practitioner community sometimes fetishises the structures over the underlying principles
- Doesn't have a strong philosophy for navigating conflict or power

## Eastern Fit

Good. The structures themselves are culturally adaptable, though the framing is very Anglo-American. Growing adoption in Asia and Latin America.

## H2GE Relevance

Very high. The modular, open-source, combinable nature mirrors our federated approach. Individual structures could be embedded in the "event-in-a-box" toolkit — local hosts pick and combine what works for their context. 1-2-4-All is already close to how Pass the Leopard works.
