# Art of Hosting (AoH)

**Toke Paludan Møller, Tenneson Woolf, Chris Corrigan, et al.**

---

Less a method than a meta-practice — a "chaordic" weaving of OST, World Café, Appreciative Inquiry, and council practices, held together by a philosophy of hosting conversations that matter. The practitioner is a host, not a facilitator. Includes a strong emphasis on inner preparation and "harvesting" collective wisdom.

## Strengths

- Philosophically coherent
- Strong practitioner learning culture
- The hosting metaphor is powerful
- Genuinely open-source and globally distributed

## Weaknesses

- Can feel like a somewhat closed tribe
- The synthesis risks being less than the sum of its parts
- Less rigorous academically

## Eastern Fit

High. Has active communities in South and SE Asia, strong in Japan and Korea.

## H2GE Relevance

Very high. The hosting metaphor is essentially what we're building — Hitchhikers as hosts of conversations that matter, not facilitators who control outcomes. The chaordic weaving of multiple methods is exactly what our event methodology needs. The harvesting practice connects to our guide-creation and documentation pipeline.
