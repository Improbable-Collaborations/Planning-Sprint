# Open Space Technology (OST)

**Harrison Owen, 1985**

---

Arguably the purest expression of self-organisation in facilitation. A convener sets a theme, participants write their own agenda on the wall, sessions run simultaneously, and the "Law of Two Feet" gives anyone permission to leave any session that isn't serving them. Bumblebees cross-pollinate between groups; Butterflies create space for serendipitous conversation.

## Strengths

- Radical trust in emergence
- Works at scale (20–2,000+)
- Extremely low facilitation overhead once running
- Produces genuine ownership
- Proceedings are usually self-documented

## Weaknesses

- Terrifying for hierarchical organisations
- Can reproduce existing power dynamics if not carefully convened
- The convener's framing question carries enormous weight — a poorly formed question can derail everything
- Not suited for situations requiring a predetermined output

## Eastern Fit

Moderate-high. The self-organising principle resonates with certain Buddhist and Daoist ideas of non-interference. Has been used in India (notably in large NGO and government contexts). The "marketplace" metaphor travels well.

## H2GE Relevance

High. The self-organising principle maps directly to our federated event model. The Law of Two Feet mirrors the spirit of hitchhiking — you go where the energy takes you. Strong candidate for the Annual Galactic Meeting format.
