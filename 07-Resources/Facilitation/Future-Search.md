# Future Search

**Marvin Weisbord & Sandra Janoff, 1987**

---

A 3-day large-group conference (64 people max, in mixed stakeholder tables) that moves through five phases: past (mind map of global trends), present (owning what we've created), future (ideal scenarios), common ground, and action planning. The whole system in the room is the organising principle.

## Strengths

- Highly structured with deep methodology behind it
- Creates genuine common ground across difference
- Produces actionable commitments

## Weaknesses

- The 64-person limit isn't truly large-scale
- Expensive and time-intensive
- The structure can feel rigid
- Less suited to emergence

## Eastern Fit

Moderate. Has been used in India and the Middle East in community development contexts.

## H2GE Relevance

Medium. The "whole system in the room" principle is valuable for our constitutional work. The 64-person limit and 3-day format don't suit our distributed model, but the phase structure (past → present → future → common ground → action) could inform the Annual Galactic Meeting design.
