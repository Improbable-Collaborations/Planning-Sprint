# Theory U

**Otto Scharmer, MIT, 2006 — built on Goethean science, Steiner, Varela**

---

A process of collective sensing and presencing — moving from downloading (reacting from habit) down through seeing, sensing, and presencing (the bottom of the U), then back up through crystallising, prototyping, and performing. The key innovation is the idea that the quality of attention determines the quality of outcome. Large-scale applications use "Societal Presencing Theatre," listening journeys, and sensing expeditions.

## Strengths

- Deeply philosophical and psychologically rich
- Excellent at shifting organisational culture, not just outputs
- The presencing concept is genuinely novel in Western facilitation
- Strong institutional backing (Presencing Institute, MIT)

## Weaknesses

- Can feel abstract or even quasi-spiritual in ways that alienate pragmatists
- The U shape risks becoming a template people perform rather than inhabit
- Top-down institutional bias in many deployments
- The "downloading" framing can feel condescending to participants

## Eastern Fit

High in theory. Scharmer draws explicitly on Zen, Vedic, and Indigenous traditions. In practice, the MIT/corporate framing creates tension when deployed in the Global South. Has been used in China and India with mixed results — the institutional scaffolding often doesn't translate.

## H2GE Relevance

Medium-high. The presencing concept aligns with Hitchhiker's Circles (Nicole's format). The listening journeys could inform our guide-creation methodology. The institutional framing needs stripping away.
