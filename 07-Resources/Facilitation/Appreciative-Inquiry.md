# Appreciative Inquiry (AI)

**David Cooperrider & Suresh Srivastva, Case Western, 1987**

---

Starts from the assumption that organisations move in the direction of what they study — so studying problems entrenches problems, and studying strengths amplifies strengths. The 4D cycle: Discover (what gives life?), Dream (what might be?), Design (what should be?), Destiny/Deploy (what will be?). Large-scale "AI Summits" can run 500–2,000 people simultaneously.

## Strengths

- Powerfully reframes organisational identity
- Generates genuine hope and momentum
- Works well in appreciative, relational cultures
- Has a strong evidence base

## Weaknesses

- Can feel naively positive — the "no deficit language" rule frustrates people with real grievances
- Not equipped to handle deep conflict or structural injustice
- The Pollyanna criticism has merit in certain settings (Cooperrider has increasingly acknowledged this)

## Eastern Fit

Very high in many Eastern contexts — aligns with positive community storytelling traditions, Ubuntu philosophy, Indian seva framing. Cooperrider's work has been applied extensively in India (Tata, Indian Railways).

## H2GE Relevance

High for the "Don't Panic" framing — the positive reframe of doom scrolling. The 4D cycle could structure our guide-creation process: Discover what communities already know → Dream what could be → Design the guide → Deploy it.
