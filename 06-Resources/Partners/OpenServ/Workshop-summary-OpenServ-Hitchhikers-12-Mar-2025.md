## 1. Where things stand

- **Alignment:** Strong agreement on the big picture (venture studio, AI + builders, Hitchhikers brand).
- **Tension:** Budget size, timelines, and how to run it (online vs in-person, scale, who runs what).

David framed two paths: (1) **proper production budget** and do it "properly" (partners, brand, universities), or (2) **minimal budget**, mainly **online**, Hitchhiker‑branded prizes, scrappy.

---

## 2. OpenServe's position (Tim)

- **Preferred:** Primarily **online** format, with the option of a few physical bookends (opener/closer or ~3 physical events).
- **Rationale:** Fits budget, capacity, and time; matches how they usually run events.
- **Audience:** Their experience is **AI builders with crypto‑adjacency** (vibecoding, agents, etc.)—that's their funnel and channels (Ethereum, Base, Discord, etc.).
- **Assumption:** Mix of OpenServe outreach + Hitchhikers community/channels. Hitchhikers is currently **stealth** (no official socials yet).

---

## 3. Hitchhikers' position (David)

- **Target:** ~**80% people who've never heard of Hitchhikers**; use the brand to reach new audiences, not only existing fans.
- **Channels:** Universities, Impact Hubs, regenerative/planet narrative, knowledge‑transfer hubs, venture studios, Buckminster Fuller Institute (partner), LiveAid‑type partners, celebrities (e.g. Vitalik, Audrey Tang). **Not** "generic crypto"—he wants to avoid ICO/crypto‑spam associations.
- **Positioning:** European/sovereign, open models, Commons, "plucky European startup." Token/ICO language is a **warning flag** for university/incubator positioning; venture studio / AI / sovereignty language is preferred.
- **Elon Musk:** Treated as a **no‑go** for current go‑to‑market and brand.

---

## 4. University strategy

- **David's ideal:** A **Hitchhiker Venture Studio** as a **remote, embeddable** program that knowledge‑transfer offices and universities can drop into existing programs (eventually an app). Pair that with **challenge prizes** (e.g. [challenge.earth](http://challenge.earth)‑style), branded and lightweight.
- **Timing:** Realistic **launch** at start of academic year (e.g. **September**); Burning Man as possible launch moment. Southern Hemisphere could be used to get around academic calendar.
- **Contacts:** David has links to Oxford, Cambridge, MIT, Stanford (e.g. Robbie as Cambridge fellow; legal/constitutional fellow at Oxford) but not a full "robust production" machine yet. Strategy: build a list of knowledge‑transfer hubs, reach out, then **go to student unions** with the story.
- **Leverage:** If they can show **global reach** (e.g. Africa, many countries), elite universities are more likely to engage (equity, diversity, PR) and may support for free or low cost.

---

## 5. Production options and budget

Two concrete production leads from David:

- **Tobias:** Ran Cardano hackathon in Africa, fully online, many events and teams. Previously ~$25k; now in the **~$42k** range. Global (South America, India, London, Switzerland).
- **Gide:** Long‑running AI hackathon circuit across **African universities (26 countries)**, 7 years, last event ~3k in person; large existing student/group network.

**Rough ballpark:** **$50k–$100k** in production/facilitation to get "hundreds of online events." An **18‑month, full‑send** version would be more like spending a meaningful slice of the builders fund (e.g. "spend your million") rather than small one‑off experiments.

---

## 6. Branding and storytelling

- David's view: Same **product** (venture studio, challenge prizes, AI, sovereignty) can be told in different "brands": Hitchhikers (playful), Buckminster Fuller / Spaceship Earth (serious), etc. **Don't** necessarily put the whole million into Hitchhikers only; position Hitchhikers within a **wider** strategy ([challenge.earth](http://challenge.earth), LiveAid‑type partners, etc.).
- He'd want a **proper** strategy and branding exercise (he compared it to ~£100k consultancy level) and clear **go‑to‑market** and audience before committing to a full workshop/plan. He's open to a **lightweight online Hitchhiker‑branded challenge** if it's clearly "save the world" and avoids crypto toxicity, but it's trickier without a joined‑up not‑for‑profit + commercial plan.

---

## 7. Reciprocity and clarity

- David is willing to send a **list of activations and contacts** (university partners, Tobias, Gide, etc.) but wants **reciprocity**: either paid strategy work or a clear exchange (e.g. joint pitch, shared effort). He's protective of that network and doesn't want to do several days of structuring workshops, stakeholder mapping, and branding without knowing **what OpenServe actually wants** (lightweight vs full 18‑month, budget envelope, timeline).
- **Ask to OpenServe:** Share a **builders fund breakdown** (production, marketing, prizes, timeline) and clarify whether this is "lightweight online" or "full 18‑month global" so Hitchhikers can respond with a costed, branded proposal.

---

## 8. Max's contributions

- **Format:** HelpBank-style **"doorbell of dreams"**—physical pop‑ups where you ring a doorbell and get a guided, branded experience; could be replicated for Hitchhikers + OpenServe.
- **Partners:** Superteam (and Cap) and **Colosseum** (big Solana hackathon) as a possible **sidetrack**; Richard Branson‑type connections via Superteam.
- **Tech / product:** **Interoperable guides** so content and journeys work together across the experience. **Privacy Mage / Mitchell:** knowledge graph that captures **journey** (conversations, how people use others' work), not just outputs; proof‑of‑understanding, sovereign identity, AI‑compressed "proverbs"; workshops → instant knowledge graph/wiki and IP protection. David tied this to the **Hitchhiker Venture Studio** stack (identity, trust, workshops → business plan/knowledge graph). **Holonic guides** with **celestial visualisation** of holonic structures—mentioned as a differentiator.

## 10. One-line takeaway

**Align on one path (lightweight online vs full university/global production), share the builders fund breakdown and timeline, and make the next move reciprocal (strategy/contacts in return for clarity or paid work)** so both sides can decide whether to go for a quick Hitchhiker‑branded online challenge or a larger, costed 18‑month proposal.
