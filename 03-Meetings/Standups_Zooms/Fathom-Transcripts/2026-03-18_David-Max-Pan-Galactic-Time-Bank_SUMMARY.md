## What it is

The world's first **global time bank** — focused on digital work (guides, coding, audio, research, teaching) rather than the hyper-local community service model of classical time banks. David spent 10 years in the traditional time bank world; this is a deliberate departure.

---

## The Pledge Ceremony (onboarding)

- Joining = pledging **42 hours** (your skills, what you want to learn/explore)
- The pledge is deposited as **audio or video** into a public "pledge bank" vault (pledgebank.org / pledgebank.earth)
- In return you get **42h of credit** — effectively an overdraft backed by your pledge
- Want more credit? Pledge more. Want to crowdfund a project? Get your community to pledge

---

## How credits flow

1. **Personal account** — you start with 42h credit to spend
2. **Invest in a guide** — e.g. donate 20h to Hitchhiker's Guide to Democracy, keep 22h for yourself
3. **Guide account** — the project accumulates hours; the project manager (the "Hitchhiker") spends them back to contributors
4. **Overall balance = zero** — it's mutual credit, not a speculative currency

## Pre-launch target: 42 Million Hours

Big marketing campaign before launch — crowdfund pledges at scale. Targets:
- **Individuals** via community events, storytelling, the improbable lottery prize
- **Companies** — law firms, blockchain foundations, agile/PM firms, VCs, startups (pledge employee time)
- **Traditional time banks** — hundreds of thousands worldwide; recruit brokers and their members

## Work that earns time credits

- Writing/researching guides
- Audio editing for guide podcasts
- Coding or vibe-coding guide tools
- Graphic design and illustration
- Teaching & mentoring (earn by teaching; spend by learning)
- Field research and interviews

## The Improbable Lottery

Fabulous pledges ("I'll pick you up at Heathrow with daffodils," "stay in my uncle's glass penthouse in Tokyo") + a €42k prize pot = **42 curated round-the-world adventure prizes** per year. Also: mentorship prizes — application-based, backed by specific business/technical/creative support. Positive feedback loop: prizes attract pledges, pledges fund more prizes.

---

## Dynamic Equity & Guides

Three options when creating a guide:

| Model | Description |
|---|---|
| **Commons** | Public good; any revenue funds Hitchhiker infrastructure |
| **Tithe (4.2%)** | Make money, use Hitchhiker IP/characters; pay 4.2% of revenue back |
| **Private spin-off** | Full commercialisation; no Hitchhiker IP; no tithe |

**Pivoting from time bank → dynamic equity:** A team running a guide on time credits can vote to commercialise. Time-bank hours *may* convert to equity shares (via Slicing the Pie) — but there must be **no guaranteed tie** to preserve legal separation and the time bank's non-commercial nature.

## Technical Strategy: Radically Simple

- Core record = a **tiny text file / JSON** on your phone (last ~100 transactions with people you trust)
- No central database needed for the basic flow
- **Clearing ceremony** — periodic (e.g. weekly): all members submit their ledger; the system flags mismatches; manager reviews, presses a button → digitally signed snapshot
- That snapshot is the durable record; optionally stored on a distributed ledger / OASIS attestation
- Approach: **Web2-style messaging** day-to-day → **Web3 ceremony** for clearing and long-term records

---

## Time Brokers

Pan-galactic version of the traditional time bank broker role — supported by agentic AI:
- Scour the pledge database for matches (e.g. 400 people interested in birds → propose a birdwatching guide)
- 200 volunteer time brokers recruited from existing time bank networks worldwide
- Earn time credits for brokering

---

## Key tensions / decisions flagged

- Classical time bank community may object ("this isn't a real time bank") — David is comfortable with that
- Legal separation of time bank from equity is critical — no guaranteed conversion
- Balance: how many guide models to launch with; when to add tithe and private options
