## 35:45 – Ahmed (Jade): Ardenova & the NEOS Operating System

**Ahmed:**
I'm sorry I'm not on my PC right now. I'm doing this from my phone, but I shared some resources in the chat — two videos.

The second one is the current state of **Ardenova**. The whole idea behind Ardenova is that it's a social media platform for doing. People spend a lot of their time on social media just browsing content, talking to each other, and getting into useless debates. But the idea here is that we're turning this into a platform for people making proposals — they see a problem, they present how they're going to solve it. They break it down into what domain this problem is in, how much it's going to cost, how much time they think it's going to take. They plan the project out in like a sprint/epic/sprint, agile scrum methodology. AI agents would be able to help work people through all of these elements and would actually just generate a lot of that stuff for them.

Then they post a proposal similar to the way somebody would on Upwork or a crowdfunding platform. So it kind of merges the idea of a freelance platform and a crowdfunding platform together — where you don't actually have to have the money to pay people right away up front. You can just have a really good idea and really want to get it done, want to get a team together, get the assets for it, get the support for it, get it in front of people. So it's kind of all in one — you're marketing it, you're presenting it, you're planning it — and it's done in a way where it feels like a very slick social media platform. I kind of modeled it after X or Blue Sky.

Once the project is posted and the funds and time have been determined, that fund is kind of fractionalized. It's now a tokenized project — an ICO essentially. I really don't see much of a difference between tokenization and the creation of an ICO; the only difference is that it's applying itself to a project instead of a specific asset. But human ideas, human time, people's efforts — that is an asset.

The way an investor would come in or one of the founders would put their efforts into it: some of that gets locked into the project all the way until it gets all the funding it needs within the timeframe allotted. If it doesn't reach it, everything is returned. Once it reaches past a certain threshold, people can go in — "we have the money now, we've got the consent of the community" — and assign tasks to people. People take those tasks, and they can take the token as a stake for the actual work and time they're putting in. They can use it to pay their bills, cash it in to the platform token. It's kind of a two-token model: each project gets its own token, but the platform has its token, and all the tokens can be exchanged with that. The thing that cashes out to fiat would be the platform token — that's the most simple way of doing it.

There's also going to be a game SDK woven into this where all of these tokens can be exchanged with each other and used in a game or application. The idea is to take the status games out of it in the real world — the problem gets solved, the value is generated, that's where it comes from. But I've come to realize there's somewhat of a basal human urge in a lot of people to have that competition, that kind of exchange process. I want to take that out of the real world and push it into a digital realm where people can have these battles, fulfill their needs for conquest, fulfill their needs to have a collectible that nobody else does, fulfill those social needs. By abstracting them onto these digital ecosystems — be it a game, be it a storytelling mechanic — that's where I saw some of the work that Oasis is doing being really, really valuable.

**The three layers of Ardenova:**
1. **Crowdfunding** — post a project, get funded
2. **Freelance/stake** — workers come on and work for a stake, reusable in a circular economy or cashed out
3. **Circular economy** — platform token enabling exchange across projects, keeping things in flow

**NEOS Operating System:**
This is the agent system I built for this. It's a New Earth Operating System, coming from somebody who's been in this space for a very long time — Josh Pasmore. It does ecosystem/community building operating systems focused not on the economic aspect — kind of a separation of church and state, where this is the statecraft, the consent-based agreement space.

The way the agent works: there are 54 skills specifically for how to interact with all the different components of the system. You have ecosystems at the top, domains underneath those ecosystems, then agreements — what are the agreements that make that domain operate and cycle? Then proposals and decisions. The AI makes it very simple to move through. It's tapped into platform data so you can ask it a question like, "What kind of agreements do people have about this?" and it can go and search it within the specific ecosystem or domain and make recommendations.

It's all consent-based. You're not forced to participate in anything. A person that's part of an ecosystem is onboarded by the initial creator — the creator goes through a process to become a coach — and eventually the goal is to make it so that everybody's kind of a coach. What defines a coach is how well they understand the system.
