## 2. Non-negotiables and fixed requirements

### 2.1 TimeBank and dynamic equity

- Every group we run with BuildersFund and hackathons **must** have TimeBank capability. It is the basis for investing in groups and spinning them off.
- **Default for all hackathon attendees and event members:** time bank, **legally** tied to dynamic equity. Not optional.
- Requirements: legally robust, correct, graphable, able to stand in a court of law; time bank linked to dynamic equity. Then we can layer risk, insurance, assets, commons, fractionalisation, etc.
- **Implementation approach:** Clone existing legal contracts and smart contracts. Add a clearing mechanism for time that is attestable by members and has legal provenance under managerial control. Then add flexibility to tweak algorithms — start simple, then vibe-code new smart contracts for advanced use cases.
- Build on existing work (tens of thousands of startups, lawyers in many jurisdictions); make it available to everyone in both not-for-profit and for-profit contexts, starting with fairness (Fair Share Commons, governance, Hitchhikers brand, mutual ownership, “fairest way to divide value” à la Slicing Pie). Architect so it can evolve into fractional pooling, liquidity pools, IP governance, and fit the longer-term “Earth operating system” vision.

### 2.2 OpenServe and BuildersFund

- **OpenServe (Tim, CEO):** A proportion or all of their Builders Fund hackathon can be Hitchhiker-directed — we specify facilitation, technical infrastructure, pricing, marketing, production.
- **Alignment:** Take OpenServe’s KPIs but be transparent and assertive about what we want to build; map the two so it’s win-win. Their objectives (e.g. agent bills) may differ slightly — document and align.
- **Package we offer:** Oasis stack at the centre; Holonic architecture; OpenServe agentic venture studio; **plus time bank as an additional wallet layer** so projects can run even without cash; link time bank monitoring to equity in incubated ventures. Use their AI stack; they get stack adoption, incubated projects, data/metrics for their venture-building and AI stack, and 3–10x attendance vs another partner.

### 2.3 Data and technical posture

- **Holonic data structure:** Single data structure readable by humans and agents — essential for OpenServe (AI agents) and for hackathons. Future: zero-knowledge proof capability.
- **Short-term build:** Prefer **not** starting on-chain. Use a standard database or flat file that is transparent and easy for us and agents; only move to smart contracts once interface, workflow, and legal are pinned down. OASIS can switch back-end data models; parts should be easily swappable (data sovereignty).
- **On-chain:** No need to go on-chain until we’re paid to run blockchain-oriented hackathons (e.g. co-branded, specific chain). Then a small team (e.g. Max) can “attach” and add Solana NFTs, smart contracts, etc. Secondary conversation with blockchain foundations who may want this on-chain (e.g. LaunchBoard as new RWA on their chain).

## 3. Product and UX principles

- **Why many similar projects fail:** Bad user experience; no one uses them. So alongside time ↔ dynamic equity design, we need a much better interface than we’ve ever built.
- **User experience first:** Speak in user concepts; prototype the flow; only when that experience is right do we touch blockchain. Avoid leading with tokenization, treasury, etc. — e.g. “synchronise” ownership (real-time) rather than “tokenise” in user-facing language.
- **Unusual requirements:** Usable in a café, on a Zoom, in group processes or alone — **voice**, “digital napkin,” as convenient as “wipe, swipe, phone, let’s make a deal.”
- **Composable, multi-audience:** Build composable parts and multiple front-ends. Different audiences use different language (e.g. stewardship vs ownership, economic rights vs governance); the Hitchhiker-facing app may use none of the same terms as internal/partner tools. Don’t assume one app (e.g. “onboard everybody to Harish’s app”).

## 4. What we’re building (positioning)

- **Open ownership infrastructure for everyone:** Programmable ownership (or equivalent framing) for any community project — “for the planet.” Shared ownership structures are expensive and hard to track for most groups; we’re solving that.
- **Not “just hackathons”:** Follow-up, venture studio, onboarding, 42-day program (sponsored or self-paced). Story: “You come here; this is how you take action, change something, make something; these are the tools and support.” Time bank ties into crowdfunding a minimum of “42 million hours” of mentorship, donation, expertise for follow-up; time credits can be earned, won, invested and used for follow-up alongside cash. Key differentiator: **curation** — we’re selecting which projects get crowdsourced/crowd-built (venture program, not one-off hackathon).
- **Reusable unit:** One (or two) structured workshop(s), self-organisable, with the mechanisms we’re defining. Market it well, seed in a target market, prove quality so people want the next one and the follow-up. Then scale (millions of events globally is the aspiration). Get the “atom” right first.
