## 16:00 – Three Graphs, One Identity

**Mitchell Travers (Soulbis):**
The 3D model that Braid added maps to the **3Graphs1Identity** concept. The three graphs are:

1. **Knowledge Graph** — your memory, like our experience that our brain holds. Agents don't have two hemispheres; they just have one, so agents can always see the entire graph, but can't traverse it without a human making the choice. A knowledge graph can be as complex as someone's brain, or as simple as a gardening club's book with a list of all their plants and the time of year they bloom — specific to that ecosystem substrate.

2. **Promise Graph** — the agent or the agency or the bilateral community. This is where promise theory comes in, and that's what draws the lines. An agent can only ever promise its own behavior, and a promise is more powerful in building trust than an imposition. The difference: if I impose that I'm going to commit to a Git repo and then don't — your trust in me collapses completely. If I promise to make a commit and then don't — there's a degradation of trust to the previous level, but also an opportunity for me to explain why. But if I promise, I do it, promise again, do it again — the trust compounds. And if I then miss a promise, like missing the Hitchhikers meeting last night when my sister just had a baby — there's a degradation, but there's also reason that can be explained. Whereas impositions (which centralization and government tend to create) have no such nuance.

3. **Trust Graph** — the intersection of all three, and we all have our own trust graph. This is the sovereign important part. We can navigate as many knowledge graphs as we want, make as many promises as we want, but our trust graph is this emergent quality that results from the overlap. And it becomes quite special when everyone gets their own trust graph represented digitally — because we do that naturally anyway in life. You and I have made a whole bunch of promises to this community and followed through, so we trust each other more.

Now replace our sovereign humanness with agents — a Max agent and a Mitchell agent — and those agents would then have to go through the path of the experience that you and I have had to create that emergence of trust between our agents. The overlap between the trust that agents get with each other is actually a reflection of the promises and knowledge that we share in overlap with each other.

What I love about it is it's two birds, one stone: agents can trust each other more, and that whole experience gives **proof of personhood** — without necessarily being a single imposition. By contrast, WorldID scanning an eyeball is a single imposition where you disclose your iris to create a proof that you're a unique human — that's just one knowledge graph. But if we can create promise graphs that overlap with that, and the trust graph emerges, then that iris scan is a single node. Instead of disclosing that proof in isolation, you disclose the coordinates or the trajectory of one of these dots in your private ledger. The knowledge graph, your private substrate, was a ZKP of an iris scan — and then you went on your own journey and created all your own overlaps with a whole bunch of different knowledge graphs. That constellation of coordinates and path is way more unique and way more valuable when selectively disclosed to anyone, because it creates discovery with zero knowledge. And then we're not relying on impositions to create personhood — we just are.
