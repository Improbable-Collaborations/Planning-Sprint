## 6:53 – David Introduces the Fabulous Machine

**David Bovill:**
The Fabulous Machine is a long time in gestation, but over the last couple of years, working with the Hitchhiker's story, the world, the characters in it, and figuring out how to use that story to enable humanity to imagine the future that it deserves.

I've worked for a long time in blockchain — I think we did the first hackathon for Holochain, about six months before the community token launch. Before that, I worked from 1993 with virtual theater performers — a background in community participation and storytelling around new forms of currency, new forms of democracy.

The hardest thing has always been communicating the interface between the human, the gatherings that people have, the belief that a new kind of future is possible, the trust in it. The opportunity of using science fiction and the humor in Hitchhikers — to imagine the future we want and then use a backcasting methodology to bring it to the present and create fixed principles. Steps of what we can co-create together in which human-centered governance meets the wondrous capabilities of blockchain and AI.

The Fabulous Machine is a way of capturing that future which humanizes it. The usual fears that people have with regard to blockchain or AI — its mechanistic, immutable harshness, the priesthood that only understands it, the inability for ordinary citizens to engage in the design process — we want to address all of that.

The aim is to co-design a Fabulous Machine based on human specifications. We run workshops with the community, small assemblies, citizen assembly-style micro-assemblies, and we use that to drive the specification. Then we bring in the experts — from the governance perspective and the technical perspective — to do iterative developments, to elaborate this Fabulous Machine over a full year.

The process of design is slow, deliberative, human-centered. We're looking at the four intelligences: human intelligence, collective intelligence, natural intelligence, artificial intelligence — bringing those together. Using science fiction as the method for us to imagine what's possible, and then literally create the designs and software that implements what we need.

We aim to start a 12-month participatory design process starting on **July 4th** — humorously also the 250th declaration of interdependence — and take that forward for 12 months with a few nice rituals along the way.
