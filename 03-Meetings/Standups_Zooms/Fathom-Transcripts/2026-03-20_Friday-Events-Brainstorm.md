## Key Discussion Points

### Events Calendar & Filters

**Annual Cycle (Filter 1) — David's four anchor dates:**
- Spring: Earth Day / Hitchhiker Day (22 April) — RSA London launch
- Summer: Annual Galactic Meeting (July area)
- Autumn: Demolition Day (5 November — Guy Fawkes)
- Winter: Groundhog Day (2 February — rebooting Earth)

**Additional calendar lenses raised:**
- Burning Man (end of August) — Jai's focus
- Equinoxes and Solstices — Nameless One/Jordan's groups already doing these
- UN World Days (~60 mapped, all "done terribly" — opportunity)
- Piggybacking on conferences (Comic Con, SXSW, COP31)
- Micro/grassroots events (busking, street theatre, pop-ups)

**Consensus emerging:** Do one event brilliantly rather than many adequately.

### The Demolition Day Vision (David + Paul)
- November 5th as "the day the Earth got blown up"
- Combines: Guy Fawkes, doom scrolling, restaurant at the end of the universe
- Cathartic: celebrate the destruction, then give everyone a towel to act
- Don't Panic as the framing — playful response to existential threat

### Paul Levy's Intervention — The Anti-Hero's Journey
- The trigger for Hitchhiker's is the most important element: Arthur's world is destroyed without his consent
- This isn't a voluntary journey — the Vogons blow up your house
- Challenge: can participants "sign up" to having their world disrupted?
- Not a fan project — taking the essence of forced transformation
- Historical parallel: "collusion of mediocrity" — people who are "wretchedly content" wanting to be blown up
- The audience should include people ready to take personal responsibility and be disrupted

### David's Vision — Hybrid Decentralised Events
- Every event is hybrid: local first, but connected
- Not Zoom-based — different form of connectivity
- Sound and radio as connective tissue (Hitchhikers.fm)
- Local embodied conversation primary; messages sent like "bottles"
- Restaurant at the End of the Universe as flagship format:
  - Community food festivals and home dinner parties
  - Pan Galactic Gargle cocktails, shared menus
  - Food miles, farm to fork, local delivery
  - Same soundtrack/radio station globally
  - Don't Panic framing
- Follow-on is essential: not just vibe, but sign-up and involvement
- Pan-Galactic Time Bank integration
- Guide-and-guidance methodology: conversations stored, published as citizen assembly outputs
- Self-organising: people create their own events using the methodology
