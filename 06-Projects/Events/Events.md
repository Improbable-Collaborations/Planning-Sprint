# Hitchhiker Events

*First contact with the culture, and an invitation into the Fabulous Machine*

## 1. Types of Events

### First Contact (Street)
Engaging people who have never heard of us — in the street, in public spaces, spontaneously. The alien arrives. The hitchhiker appears. A moment of curiosity that opens a door.

- Street performance and open world theatre
- Pop-up installations
- Guerrilla guides — handing out or revealing unexpected content in public
- Earth Review moments (#EarthReview)
- Towel Day activations

### Gatherings (In-Person Venues)
Structured events in spaces — cafés, community centres, galleries, farms, the RSA.

- Restaurant at the End of the Universe — food-centred events connecting farm-to-fork with governance
- Alien Workshops — designing aliens, imagining life from outside perspectives
- Value mapping and constitutional workshops (legislative theatre)
- Book launches and guide reveals
- Stakeholder mapping sessions (Gustav-facilitated)
- Hackathons and vibe coding sprints

### Connected Events (Online + Hybrid)
Multiple locations linked live — the decentralised event model.

- 42 locations, one event, moving through time zones
- Pirate radio / global soundscape connecting local performances
- Shared screen moments — "cut to Melbourne, cut to Barcelona"
- Online-only workshops for distributed participants
- Podcast recordings and live interviews

### Milestone Events (Annual Calendar)
The rhythm of the year — recurring dates that build tradition.

## 3. How We Run Events

### Street (First Contact)
- Small team (pod of 6), costumes optional, towels mandatory
- A simple hook: a question, a game, a moment of absurdity
- Capture: photo, short video, voice note — feeds the guide and the broadcast
- Leave something behind: a card, a QR code, an invitation to the next gathering
- No hard sell — it's an encounter, not a pitch

### In-Person Venues
- Venue partnership model: we bring the content and community, they provide the space
- Standard run sheet adaptable to any location
- Tech requirements: screen/projector, internet, audio for remote connections
- Recording: Fathom or equivalent for transcription and action point extraction
- Facilitation: trained facilitators (Gustav model) or peer-facilitated (open space)

### Online / Hybrid
- Platform: Zoom (current), moving to own infrastructure
- Time zone management: events roll through zones, or anchor to one zone with async participation
- Live audio routing between locations (David's expertise)
- Shared visual workspace (Mural/Miro currently, own tools later)
- Recording and transcription pipeline (MacWhisper → Claude → repo)

### Connected Multi-Location
- Minimum: 3 locations to feel "connected"
- Target: 42 locations for milestone events
- Each location runs autonomously with a local host
- Central coordination: a "mission control" that routes audio/video between sites
- Soundscape: ambient audio from all locations woven into a live global mix
- Tested so far: Scotland, Barcelona, London, Portland — "okay, but not great" (Graham)

## 4. First Contact Design

The moment someone encounters Hitchhikers for the first time. This is not onboarding — it's the spark before onboarding.

**Principles:**
- Lead with curiosity, not explanation
- Humour disarms — use it
- Leave them wanting more, not overwhelmed
- Every first contact creates a trace: a recording, a note, a connection
- The guide is not a brochure — it's an invitation to co-create

**The question:** What does first contact look like in Melbourne vs Barcelona vs London vs Portland vs Delhi? Each embassy designs their own version.

## 6. Economics

### Costs
- Venue hire (or partnership/in-kind)
- Tech: streaming, audio routing, recording
- Materials: printed guides, towels, cards, props
- Facilitation: facilitator time (paid or time-banked)
- Travel: for key team members to anchor events
- Production: video editing, transcription, post-event content

### Revenue / Funding
- Ticket sales (sliding scale / pay-what-you-can)
- Guide sales (digital and physical)
- Sponsorship (with governance guardrails — see Robbie's ethical framework)
- Grants (arts councils, cultural funds, democracy/governance funds)
- Crowdfunding (per-event or per-season)
- Time bank contributions (non-monetary participation)
- Pangalactic Lottery (longer term)
