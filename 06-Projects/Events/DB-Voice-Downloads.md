## David's Event Strategy Layers

### Layer 1: Annual Milestone Events

Four anchor dates forming a narrative arc across the year:

| Season | Event | Theme |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Spring | **Hitchhiker Day / Earth Day** (22 April) | Planting hope |
| Summer | **The AGM** (4 July) | Constitution, prizes, summer schools |
| Autumn | **Demolition Day** (5 November) | Democracy, doom, fireworks |
| Winter | **Groundhog Day** (2 February) | Rebooting Earth from backup |

Plus an **esoteric mirror layer** — solstices and cosmologically-aligned events that shadow the four milestones but speak to a different sensibility.

Key consideration: how many milestones in year one? One done well, or all four? David suggests four is the maximum.

### Layer 2: Six-Week Workshop Cycles

- ~8 cycles per year filling ~352 days plus holidays
- Each cycle has its own internal events: opening day, weekly rhythm, closing showcase
- Closing events should align with the annual milestones (the six-week sprint leads up to the big event)
- Content from one cycle feeds into the next — podcasts, NFTs, visual releases
- Cycles should be **coordinated globally** — staggered so content from Africa feeds into London feeds into America
- Podcast release schedule tied to the cycle (weekly, staggered by region or synchronised)

### Layer 3: UN/World Days Strategy

- David created a calendar mapping ~60 UN/UNDP world days against Hitchhiker relevance
- Core insight: **these are all done terribly — massive opportunity to do them well**
- Year one: pick ~4 relevant world days and run them in Hitchhiker style
- Year two+: add more, invite self-organised groups to adopt days using shared methodology
- Examples: World Soil Day (December), World Radio Day (January), etc.

### Layer 4: Piggybacking on Existing Events

- Research major global conferences/festivals (AI conferences, space congresses, Comic Con, Smart Cities, South by Southwest, food events in India, trance festivals in Goa)
- Three modes of engagement:
  1. **Guerrilla** — buy tickets, turn up, interview people, do pop-ups (no permission needed)
  2. **Partnership** — pitch to be part of the official programme
  3. **Paid intervention** — offer to create guides, satellite events, global connections for a fee (~20k)

### Layer 5: Micro-Events / Grassroots Pop-Ups

- Individual hitchhikers organising small gatherings — online or in-person, spontaneous
- Woven into Hitchhiker Radio and the production schedule
- TikToks, audio clips, small recordings fed into the social media calendar
- Occasional live events when a venue or interesting topic emerges
- The most granular, always-on layer

### Layer 6: Astral / Celestial Events

- Meteor showers, eclipses (e.g. Catalonia, August 2026), astronomical phenomena
- Natural hooks for wonder-driven, location-specific events

### Layer 7: Historical Story-Driven Dates

- Find historical events that fit the Hitchhiker myth — constitutional moments, philosophical milestones, obscure but resonant dates
- Examples: Budapest Open Knowledge Declaration, dawn of the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth, first use of the word "robot" (Karel Čapek)
- Dates chosen for narrative power rather than existing recognition
- Fills a gap the four annual milestones don't cover — they lack deep historical resonance (4th July = American, Guy Fawkes = English/colonial, Groundhog Day = absurdist American, Towel Day = fun but shallow)
