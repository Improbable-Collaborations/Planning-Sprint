## 1. How Open Collective Works

### Core Concepts

**Open Collective (the platform)** is a software platform for transparent financial management. It is not itself a fiscal host or legal entity that holds your money. Think of it as the accounting/transparency layer.

**Collectives** are groups/projects that use the platform to raise and spend money transparently. A Collective does not need to be a legal entity. It is essentially a project page with a transparent budget.

**Fiscal Hosts** are legal entities (companies, nonprofits, foundations) that hold a Collective's funds in their bank account, handle taxes, accounting, compliance, and process approved expenses. They lend their legal status to the Collective.

**Independent Collectives** are Collectives that plug in their own bank account and Stripe account. They act as their own legal entity. This is suitable if you already have a company (like Not Panicking Ltd) and just want the transparency/fundraising tools.

### Key Distinction

- **Open Collective Inc** = the company that builds and runs the software platform
- **Open Collective Foundation (OCF)** = was a US 501(c)(3) fiscal host (NOW DISSOLVED - see USA section)
- **Open Source Collective (OSC)** = a US 501(c)(6) fiscal host for open source projects (still operating)
- **Open Collective Europe (OCE)** = a Belgian nonprofit fiscal host for European projects (still operating)

These are all separate legal entities that happen to use the same platform.

### How Transparent Budgeting Works

- All income and expenses are publicly visible on the Collective's page
- Contributors can see exactly how their money is spent
- Expense claims are submitted through the platform and approved by Collective admins
- The fiscal host then processes the payment
- Full transaction history is always visible

### Current Platform Fees (as of 2026)

Open Collective has moved to an activity-based pricing model for fiscal hosts (replacing the old 15% revenue share on host fees):

| Plan | Monthly Cost | Active Collectives Included | Paid Expenses/Month | Extra Expense Cost |
|------|-------------|---------------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Discover** | $0/month | 1 (then $10/each extra) | 10 (then $1/each extra) | $1 |
| **Basic** | $60/month | 5 (then $15/each extra) | 50 (then $1.50/each extra) | $1.50 |
| **Pro** | $320/month | 20 (then $20/each extra) | 200 (then $2/each extra) | $2 |

**For Collectives themselves:** Free to use. Supported by optional "Platform Tips" (voluntary contributions from donors) or an alternative 5% crowdfunding fee option.

**Payment processing fees** (Stripe, PayPal) are separate and charged at standard rates (typically ~2.9% + fixed fee for cards).

## 2. USA Setup

### CRITICAL: Open Collective Foundation (OCF) Has Dissolved

**OCF is no longer operating.** The Board of Directors announced dissolution effective 31 December 2024.

- **Key dates (all passed):**
  - Last day to accept funds: 15 March 2024
  - Last day for employees: 30 June 2024
  - Last day to spend/transfer funds: 30 September 2024
  - Formal dissolution: 31 December 2024

- **Reason:** The business model was not sustainable given the complex services offered and fees paid to Open Collective Inc.

- **Impact:** Over 600 collectives had to close or transition. There is no direct replacement 501(c)(3) fiscal host on the Open Collective platform for general-purpose projects.

### Current USA Options on Open Collective

| Option | Status | Type | Fee | Suitable for H2GE? |
|--------|--------|------|-----|-------------------|
| **Open Source Collective** | Operating | 501(c)(6) | 10% | No - only for open source software projects |
| **Independent Collective** | Available | Self-hosted (need own entity) | Platform fees only | Only if you have a US entity |

**The gap:** There is currently no general-purpose US 501(c)(3) fiscal host on the Open Collective platform. This is the biggest limitation for US-based fundraising.

### Alternative US Fiscal Sponsors (Off-Platform)

For a creative/cultural project like H2GE, these are the strongest options:

#### Fractured Atlas (Recommended for Creative Projects)
- **Type:** 501(c)(3) nonprofit
- **Focus:** Arts and creative projects specifically
- **Fee:** 8% administrative fee on all donations (no additional credit card processing fees)
- **Membership:** $10/month (Professional) or $20/month (Organizational, up to 3 users)
- **Tax-deductible:** Yes - donors get 501(c)(3) tax deductions
- **Grants:** Can apply for grants and corporate sponsorships (Fractured Atlas reviews/approves grant applications before submission)
- **Application:** Become a member first, then submit fiscal sponsorship application (~2 weeks for approval)
- **Website:** [fracturedatlas.org](https://www.fracturedatlas.org/fiscal-sponsorship)

#### NYFA (New York Foundation for the Arts)
- **Type:** 501(c)(3) nonprofit
- **Focus:** Individual artists, arts groups/collectives, emerging arts organisations
- **Status:** Was accepting projects on a limited basis until July 2025; check current availability
- **Tax-deductible:** Yes
- **Website:** [nyfa.org](https://www.nyfa.org/fiscal-sponsorship/)

#### Intersection for the Arts
- **Type:** 501(c)(3) nonprofit
- **Focus:** Creative projects
- **Location:** San Francisco-based
- **Application:** Has cohort-based intake (check for upcoming deadlines)
- **Website:** [theintersection.org](https://theintersection.org/get-support/fiscal-sponsorship/)

### "Friend in the USA" Option

If the friend has or creates a 501(c)(3), they could:
1. Become a fiscal host on Open Collective (using the Discover plan at $0/month for a single collective)
2. Hold funds for the H2GE US collective
3. This requires the friend's entity to handle all tax, accounting, and compliance obligations
4. **Caution:** Running a 501(c)(3) has real legal and tax obligations - this is not trivial

### US Tax Implications

- **For donors:** Donations to a 501(c)(3) fiscal sponsor are tax-deductible. Donations to a 501(c)(6) or a foreign entity are generally NOT tax-deductible.
- **For the collective:** Income received through a fiscal host is generally not taxable to the collective (it belongs to the host). Expenses/payments to individuals are reported on 1099 forms.
- **Key point:** Without a 501(c)(3) fiscal host, US donors cannot claim tax deductions, which significantly reduces fundraising potential.

## 3. UK Setup

### Available UK Fiscal Hosts on Open Collective

#### The Social Change Nest (SCN)
- **Legal structure:** Community Interest Company (CIC), company number 12611737
- **Charity status:** In the process of registering as a charity (not yet registered)
- **Focus:** Grassroots organisations, mutual aid, social impact
- **Scale:** 500+ hosted groups, distributed over GBP 23 million since 2021
- **Fees:**
  - GBP 42 one-time onboarding fee (charged after first funds received)
  - 5% on incoming funds (excluding grants)
  - 7-15% on grant management (depending on complexity)
- **Gift Aid:** NOT currently available (because they are a CIC, not a registered charity). Will become available if/when charity registration completes.
- **Grant support:** Can act as guarantor for grant applications, sign cooperation agreements, sign letters of intent
- **Application process:** Submit application, attend onboarding call, create OC page, add second admin, upload safeguarding policy if applicable. ~2 weeks minimum.
- **Requirements:** Minimum 2 administrators, evidence of activity
- **Contact:** hello@thesocialchangenest.org
- **Website:** [thesocialchangenest.org](https://thesocialchangenest.org/services-initiatives/fiscal-hosting/)

#### Huddlecraft
- **Focus:** Peer-to-peer learning, support and action; UK-based projects or GBP-currency projects
- **Fees:** 4.25% fiscal hosting fee + GBP 50 one-time setup fee
- **Note:** Smaller operation than SCN; was specifically listed as accepting former OCF projects

### Can Not Panicking Ltd Act as Its Own Fiscal Host?

**Yes.** There are two options:

**Option A: Independent Collective**
- Not Panicking Ltd connects its own bank account and Stripe account to Open Collective
- Operates as a single Collective with full transparency tools
- No fiscal host fees (only platform fees if applicable)
- Not Panicking Ltd handles all its own tax, accounting, and compliance
- Best if you only need one collective/budget

**Option B: Become a Fiscal Host**
- Not Panicking Ltd registers as a fiscal host on Open Collective
- Can then host multiple collectives (e.g., different H2GE sub-projects)
- Can set its own host fee structure
- Platform fees apply based on the plan tier chosen
- Requirements: Stripe account, PayPal account, bank account, admin capacity

**Advantages of using Not Panicking Ltd directly:**
- No third-party fees (beyond platform/payment processing)
- Full control over funds and decisions
- Can receive funds immediately (no application process)
- Can accept grants directly as a UK Ltd company

**Disadvantages:**
- Not Panicking Ltd is a Ltd company, not a charity or CIC
- Cannot claim Gift Aid on donations
- Donors cannot claim charitable tax relief
- Less attractive for grant funders who prefer charitable entities
- All accounting/compliance burden falls on the company

### UK Entity Structure Considerations

| Structure | Gift Aid | Charitable Grants | Tax Relief | Regulatory Burden | Setup Time |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|------------|-------------------|------------|
| **Ltd Company** (e.g., Not Panicking Ltd) | No | Limited (some funders accept, many don't) | Corporation tax on profits | Low | Already exists |
| **CIC** (Community Interest Company) | No | Most grant funders accept CICs | Corporation tax on profits (like any Ltd) | Medium (asset lock, CIC report) | ~2-4 weeks to convert or register new |
| **CIO** (Charitable Incorporated Organisation) | Yes (+25%) | Full access to charitable grants | Exempt from corporation tax on charitable activities | Higher (Charity Commission regulation) | 2-6 months to register |
| **Charitable Company** (CLG + charity) | Yes (+25%) | Full access | Exempt from corporation tax on charitable activities | Highest (Companies House + Charity Commission) | 2-6 months |

**Key insight:** If H2GE plans to rely significantly on UK donations and grants, a charitable structure (CIO or charitable company) provides much better financial advantages through Gift Aid alone (25% uplift on every donation). However, this takes months to set up and brings regulatory obligations.

**Pragmatic approach:** Not Panicking Ltd can start receiving funds immediately. Consider converting to a CIC or registering a separate CIO in parallel if longer-term charitable fundraising is the plan.

### HMRC Considerations

- **Not Panicking Ltd:** Standard corporation tax obligations. Income from donations is still taxable income unless the company has charitable status.
- **VAT:** If Not Panicking Ltd is VAT-registered (or exceeds the threshold), VAT implications apply to certain income streams. Donations and grants are generally outside the scope of VAT, but sponsorship income may be subject to VAT.
- **Gift Aid:** Only available to HMRC-recognised charities and CASCs. A Ltd company or CIC cannot claim it.

## 4. Europe / EU Setup

### Open Collective Europe (OCE)

**The primary option for EU-based fundraising.**

- **Legal structure:** Belgian Foundation of Public Utility (Fondation d'utilite publique), registered in Brussels
- **Location:** Cantersteen 10, 1000 Brussels, Belgium
- **Scale:** 500+ groups, processes EUR 35M+ annually across its network
- **Contact:** oce@opencollective.com
- **Website:** [oceurope.org](https://www.oceurope.org/)

#### Fees

| Type | Fee |
|------|-----|
| Online contributions (Stripe/PayPal) | 8% |
| Bank transfers | 10% |
| Tax-deductible donations and grants | 10% |

(Plus standard payment processor fees on top)

#### What They Host

- Open source communities
- Solidarity initiatives
- Civic tech projects
- **Cultural and artistic projects** (explicitly mentioned in their strategy)
- Climate and democracy initiatives
- Projects from "garden collectives to global democracy initiatives"
- International projects (including Africa)

#### Grant Support

- **Can sign cooperation agreements** for collectives to receive grants and public subsidies
- **European subsidies:** All hosted collectives can apply regardless of country
- **Belgian subsidies:** Belgium-based collectives can also apply for national/regional subsidies
- **Regranting:** OCE manages philanthropic funds and regranting mechanisms for foundations

#### Tax-Deductible Donations

- **US donors:** Tax-deductible (OCE has 501(c)(3) equivalency)
- **Belgian donors:** Tax-deductible
- **Other EU countries:** Varies by country; OCE works with partners including the King Baudouin Foundation to enable tax deductibility in multiple jurisdictions
- Contact OCE to verify specific country eligibility

#### Application Process

1. Submit an expression of interest via their Apply section
2. Read and agree to Terms of Fiscal Hosting
3. Project must align with nonprofit mission (no private benefit)
4. Cannot engage in political/religious campaigning
5. Projects retain full ownership of IP and brand

#### Key Benefits

- Can send money to 60+ countries
- Virtual cards tied to budgets
- Expense reimbursement typically within one week
- No need to create your own legal entity

### Other EU Fiscal Hosts on Open Collective

#### All For Climate
- **Focus:** Climate and social justice initiatives in Europe
- **Fee:** 3%
- **Note:** Very low fee but narrow focus area

### Erasmus+ Grant Compatibility

**This is complex. Key findings:**

- Erasmus+ generally requires applicants to be **legal entities** established in an EU Member State or associated third country
- An exception exists for **informal groups of young people** (at least 4 members, one aged 18-30 acts as legal representative) - mainly for youth-field actions
- **Through Open Collective Europe:** OCE is a legal entity (Belgian foundation) that can sign cooperation agreements for grants. Collectives hosted by OCE can apply for European subsidies through OCE acting as the legal applicant/signatory.
- **Important caveat:** Whether OCE can be the formal applicant for a specific Erasmus+ action depends on the call requirements. Some actions require specific types of organisations (universities, schools, training providers). For broader cultural/creative actions under Erasmus+, it may be possible with OCE as the legal entity.
- **Recommendation:** Contact OCE directly to discuss Erasmus+ eligibility for H2GE's specific project type before assuming this will work.

### VAT Implications (EU)

- Donations and grants are generally outside the scope of VAT
- If the collective engages in trading/commercial activities, VAT may apply
- Belgian VAT rules apply to OCE as the legal entity
- Cross-border services within the EU may trigger reverse charge mechanisms
- Sponsorship income is typically subject to VAT

### Cross-Border Considerations

- OCE operates under Belgian law but can host projects from any country
- Payments to individuals in other EU countries are processed through the platform (Wise, PayPal, bank transfer)
- Tax reporting obligations may vary depending on where expense recipients are based
- Double taxation treaties may be relevant for payments across borders

| Jurisdiction | For the Collective | For Donors |

| **EU (OCE)** | Funds held by OCE; OCE handles tax | Tax-deductible in some countries |

### Banking/Payment Methods

All Open Collective options support:
- **Incoming:** Credit/debit cards (Stripe), PayPal, bank transfers
- **Outgoing/Expenses:** Bank transfer (via Wise), PayPal, virtual cards (some hosts)

Fractured Atlas has its own payment infrastructure (credit cards, online donations).

### Withdrawal/Expense Process

On Open Collective:
1. Someone submits an expense (invoice, reimbursement, or grant payout)
2. Collective admin(s) approve the expense
3. Fiscal host reviews and processes payment
4. Payment sent via Wise, PayPal, or bank transfer
5. Typically processed within 1-7 business days depending on the host

### Pros/Cons of Open Collective vs Alternatives

**Open Collective Pros:**
- Full financial transparency (attractive to donors and grant funders)
- Low administrative burden (host handles compliance)
- Professional fundraising pages
- Multiple payment methods
- Community/social features
- Can switch between fiscal hosts

**Open Collective Cons:**
- Fees stack up (host fee + payment processing)
- Dependent on fiscal host availability and policies
- US landscape weakened by OCF dissolution
- Less control than running your own entity
- Some fiscal hosts have narrow eligibility criteria

## 7. Practical Recommendations for H2GE

### Fastest Path to Receiving Money in All Three Jurisdictions

#### Immediate (This Week)

**UK - Set up Not Panicking Ltd as an Independent Collective on Open Collective**
- Connect Not Panicking Ltd's bank account and Stripe account
- Create a Collective page for H2GE
- Can receive donations immediately via card and bank transfer
- Cost: Essentially free (voluntary platform tips + ~2.9% payment processing)
- Limitation: No Gift Aid, limited grant attractiveness

#### Short-Term (2-4 Weeks)

**USA - Apply to Fractured Atlas for fiscal sponsorship**
- Sign up for membership ($10-20/month)
- Submit fiscal sponsorship application
- ~2 weeks for approval
- Once approved, can receive tax-deductible donations from US donors
- Can apply for US grants through them
- Cost: 8% on donations + membership

**EU - Apply to Open Collective Europe**
- Submit expression of interest to OCE
- Discuss H2GE project scope and Erasmus+ ambitions directly with OCE
- ~2-4 weeks for approval
- Once approved, can receive donations and apply for EU grants
- Cost: 8-10% on incoming funds

#### Medium-Term (2-6 Months)

**UK - Consider charitable structure**
- If significant UK donation income is expected, the Gift Aid uplift (25%) more than offsets the effort
- Options: Register a separate CIO, or convert/create a charitable company alongside Not Panicking Ltd
- Not Panicking Ltd keeps the IP; the charity handles donations/grants
- This is a parallel track, not blocking immediate fundraising

**EU - Explore Erasmus+ eligibility**
- Work with OCE to understand which Erasmus+ actions H2GE could apply for
- Determine whether OCE can act as the legal applicant
- Consider whether a direct EU entity is needed long-term

### Summary Action Plan

| Priority | Action | Timeline | Estimated Cost |
|----------|--------|----------|---------------|
| 1 | Set up Not Panicking Ltd as Independent Collective on Open Collective | This week | Free + payment processing |
| 2 | Apply to Fractured Atlas (USA) | This week, live in 2-4 weeks | 8% + $10-20/mo |
| 3 | Apply to Open Collective Europe | This week, live in 2-4 weeks | 8-10% |
| 4 | Contact OCE re: Erasmus+ eligibility | This month | Free (enquiry) |
| 5 | Investigate UK charitable structure (CIO) | Next 1-2 months (decision) | Legal costs + 2-6 months registration |
| 6 | Explore whether friend's US entity could become a fiscal host | When convenient | Depends on entity status |

### Key Risks and Watchpoints

1. **US gap:** With OCF gone, there is no 501(c)(3) fiscal host on the Open Collective platform itself. Fractured Atlas is off-platform but proven for creative projects.
2. **Gift Aid gap:** Neither Not Panicking Ltd nor Social Change Nest can currently offer Gift Aid. This is a material gap for UK fundraising.
3. **Erasmus+ uncertainty:** Whether OCE can act as legal applicant for Erasmus+ depends on the specific call. Do not assume this works without confirming.
4. **Fee stacking:** On Open Collective, payment processing fees (2.9%) are on top of host fees (5-10%). Budget for 8-13% total cost on incoming funds.
5. **Open Collective platform pricing changes:** The platform has moved to activity-based pricing for hosts. If Not Panicking Ltd becomes a fiscal host for multiple collectives, monthly platform fees apply.
