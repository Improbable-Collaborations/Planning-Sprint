# BRAID — Identity & Trust Layer
**For Tim Hafner (OpenSERV) · March 2026**

---

## The Conceptual Layer

Mitchell Travers from Soulbis, who is building our sovereign identity and agent privacy layer, has been working directly with the BRAID research paper. Using it, he updated his *Privacy Is Value* equation to v5 and derived what he calls **3Graphs1Identity** — three overlapping graphs that together define a person's sovereign digital identity:

- **Knowledge Graph** — what someone knows and has experienced (their memory substrate)
- **Promise Graph** — the commitments they make and keep with others (draws the lines between nodes)
- **Trust Graph** — the emergent intersection of the two; unique to each person, grows more valuable over time

BRAID's three axes — **agent / data / inference** — are what gave this framework its 3D structure.

---

## Why This Matters for OpenSERV

When builders participate in a hackathon or a Pan-Galactic Time Bank session, they're not just producing outputs — they're creating nodes and drawing lines. Every promise made and kept between participants hardens a line in the trust graph. The longer someone builds in this system, the more unique and verifiable their identity trajectory becomes. That's proof of personhood that emerges organically from the work itself, without imposing a single disclosure (like an iris scan).

---

## The Proposal

Rather than funding the trust graph as a separate item, it's actually a byproduct of what we're already building. Wire Marvin on BRAID/x402, run the Builders Fund hackathons through the Pan-Galactic Monitor, and the trust graph accumulates as a native output.

Mitchell is making commits to the Improbable repo this week to formalise the identity layer — including SSH key signing via Open Integrity (Christopher Allen's setup) and DID support.
